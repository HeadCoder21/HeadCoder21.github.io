Question 1: Immigration

As you may already know, immigration in the United States is a heated topic. Still, undocumented immigrants cross the border, and the numbers have been increasing. While some people belive in strengthening the border and delegating more state and federal funds for security, others want a more open border policy, giving the ability for undocumented immigrants to cross into the United States freely.

Question 2: Climate Change

Climate change is affecting the world globally, with floods, hurricanes, extreme heat, and much more. Members in the government want to pass laws and spending measures on climate change, though others disagree and some challenge the existence of climate change. The United States participates in the Paris Climate Accords, and agreed to have zero carbon emissions by 2050. However, with the country split on this topic, there's a chance the US might miss the deadline. To make problems worse, other countries look to the US to base their climate policies.

How should the United States approach this topic? Is it their job to fix the climate crisis? Click the button with the federal climate policy you agree with.

Question 4: School Busing

When the Supreme Court demanded school integration in the 1950s, counties across the country were required to allow students of different races to share the same school. This meant some state governments and the federal government started to bus students to schools in far away locations from where they live. This led to a backlash from parents who did not want their children to commute long distances to school that still drives on today. People have proposed magnet schools and other ideas, but the problem still remains and lingers in the US.

How should the US government respond? Should they let the counties do their own thing or get directly involved? Click on the policy that you believe in the most.
